What is a Orbit Shot?

The camera travels in a circular or semi-circular path around a stationary subject, keeping the subject at centre frame. A full rotation is 360; half is 180.

When to use it

Use to give a subject full three-dimensional presence — the definitive hero shot.

Emotional effect

Heroic energy, dimensionality, importance. The subject feels earned and significant.

Prompt formula

Base phrase: a 180-degree orbit circling around the subject

Full formula: [Subject] + Camera: a 180-degree orbit circling around the subject + [Style] + [Lighting] + [Pacing] + [Ratio]

Common mistakes

  • Not specifying the orbit angle — 180 vs 360 produce very different shots.
  • Using fast pacing on an orbit — it almost always looks better slow or steady.
  • Combining with handheld style.

FAQ

Orbit vs arc?+
They describe the same move. Arc often implies partial (under 180 degrees). Both are understood by AI video models.
Specify direction?+
You can — 'orbiting clockwise' adds precision. Most models interpret orbit correctly without it.
Combine with dolly in?+
Yes. 'A slow orbit combined with a gradual dolly in' creates a cinematic spiral approach.
