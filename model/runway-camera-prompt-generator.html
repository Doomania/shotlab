About Runway prompt formatting

Runway prefers natural, cinematic prose over rigid field labels. Write as a director describing a shot — the camera movement should flow naturally within the description.

Default ratio: 16:9

Tips for Runway prompts

  • Write as a director: 'The camera slowly pushes in as the character turns.'
  • Runway handles lighting moods exceptionally well — be detailed.
  • Add 'film grain', 'anamorphic lens flare', 'shallow depth of field' for cinematic results.
  • Keep Gen-3 Turbo prompts shorter — 100–150 words is the sweet spot.
  • Avoid rigid labels like 'Camera:' — integrate movement into narrative prose.

FAQ

Should I use 'Camera:' labels in Runway prompts?+
No — Runway prefers prose. Write 'The camera slowly glides forward' rather than 'Camera: slow dolly in.'
How long should Runway prompts be?+
100–200 words for Gen-3. Longer prompts cause Runway to ignore later details.
What styles work best?+
Runway excels at photorealistic cinematic, fashion editorial, and high-budget commercial styles.
Does Runway support 9:16?+
Yes, Gen-3 supports multiple aspect ratios including 9:16.
