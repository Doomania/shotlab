About Kling prompt formatting

Kling by Kuaishou handles complex camera movement prompts well and supports multi-step compound moves. It produces high-quality motion and is strong for both cinematic and anime content.

Default ratio: 16:9

Tips for Kling prompts

  • Use complete sentences for each element.
  • Label the camera section: 'Camera movement: ...' as a distinct sentence.
  • Kling handles multi-step moves well: 'dolly in, then arc, then pull back.'
  • Include character detail and environment for best results.
  • Supports both 16:9 widescreen and 9:16 vertical.

FAQ

Best prompt structure for Kling?+
One sentence per element: subject, camera movement, style, lighting, pacing. Label the camera section clearly.
Can Kling handle compound moves?+
Yes — one of the better models for multi-step moves. Use 'then' to chain them.
Is Kling good for anime?+
Yes. Specify the anime subgenre explicitly: 'high-detail shonen anime' or 'Studio Ghibli-inspired.'
What resolution does Kling support?+
16:9 widescreen and 9:16 vertical. Default is 16:9.
