What is a Crane Up?

A vertical camera movement ascending on a mechanical arm from eye-level to a high angle, often with a sweeping arc.

When to use it

Use for grand establishing shots, climactic finales, or any moment where you want to reveal the full scale of a scene.

Emotional effect

Scale, grandeur, finality, transcendence. The viewer is elevated out of the moment.

Prompt formula

Base phrase: a crane rise to a high-angle reveal of the scene

Full formula: [Subject] + Camera: a crane rise to a high-angle reveal of the scene + [Style] + [Lighting] + [Pacing] + [Ratio]

Common mistakes

  • Pairs poorly with fast pacing — the rise needs time to register.
  • Combining with Crane Down in the same prompt.
  • Not specifying what is revealed at the top.

FAQ

Crane Up vs Pedestal Up?+
Pedestal lifts straight up like an elevator. Crane Up sweeps on an arc — grander and more cinematic.
Good for openings?+
A reverse Crane (descending) is a classic opening. Crane Up is a powerful finale or revelatory shot.
Veo format?+
'The camera performs a crane rise from eye level, ascending to reveal the full expanse below.'
